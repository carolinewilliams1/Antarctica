My Antarctic Experience

In January 2024, I left my home in New York City to go on a trip to Antarctica. I went on this trip with a dear family friend who invited me to join them on this expedition as part of a group with the Center for Ecosystem Sentinels. The Center works to understand what sentinel species are telling us, to protect their environment, and to inspire others to take action.


Our journey began with anticipation and excitement as we left behind the hustle of New York and made our way to Buenos Aires, Argentina. From there, we traveled southward to Ushuaia, a charming port city nestled amidst the peaks of the Andes. Boarding the vessel SH Diana, we set sail across the icy waters, passing through the Falkland Islands and South Georgia. Each stop along the way revealed a unique tapestry of wildlife and natural wonders.


As we explored this frozen realm, I couldn't help but be captivated by its rich history, from the brave explorers to the dedicated scientists working in research stations. Their stories and discoveries added depth to my experience, connecting me to the land in a profound way. Join me as I highlight the history of Antarctica, uncover the mysteries of its research stations, and share some moments from my personal experience.
